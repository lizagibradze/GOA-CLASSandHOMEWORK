The axolotls, also known as the Mexican walking fish, is a unique species of salamander (Ambystoma mexicanum) native to lakes near Mexico City. Unlike most amphibians,axolotls retain their juvenile features throughout their life, a trait known as neoteny. They are famous for their ability to regenerate lost limbs , organs, and even parts of their brain , making them a subject of scientific study. These aquatic creatures have feathery external gills, allowing them to breathe underwater. Axolotls are critically endangered in the wild due to habitat loss and pollution, though they are commonly bred in captivity for research and as pets.
