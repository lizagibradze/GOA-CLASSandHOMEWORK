    Deer are graceful, herbivorous mammals found worldwide, except in Antarctica. They belong to the Cervidae family, which includes species like white-tailed Deer , elk, moose, and reindeer. Most male Deer grow antlers, which they shed and regrow annually. They are social animals, often living in herds, and rely on their keen senses of hearing and smell to detect predators.Deer are adaptable, inhabiting forests, grasslands, and even urban areas. They are important to ecosystems, influencing plant growth and serving as prey for predators. Humans hunt deer for food and sport, and some species, like the reindeer, are domesticated for work.
